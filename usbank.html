Project background

What the product does

The product structure is pretty simple! Customers with an existing credit line can use the app to portion their credit into corporate cards for their employees.

Those cards can be customized with a variety of controls and limits, and monitored for reporting and compliance.

Customer profile

Our typical customer is a small business with 12-25 employees, who make work expenses on behalf of the owner. They'll typically be in healthcare or construction.

This type of customer has a lot of daily expenses that need to be tracked, organized, and reviewed.

User needs

1. Be able to instantly and easily create cards for my employees

2. Monitor activity and stop bad spend before it happens

3. Have a central place to save expenses for accounting

Our card creation process is highly configurable, allowing users to set custom limits and restrictions on types of transactions.

Product goals

Deepen customer relationships

Our product led the way in developing customer primacy (who is your "main" bank).

The more time people spend with our product, the more likely they are to rely on the bank to handle their cash. Our users spend 3.5x more dollars with U.S. Bank than typical customers.

Drive user adoption

The product creates revenue via credit interchange fees. Thus, features were geared around encouraging daily usage.

Our card creation and management features had to be lightweight and easy to understand.

Design to scale

After acquisition, our customerbase increased dramatically. I designed flows to be unbreakable no matter the entrypoint, customer size, or account type.

The size and variance of our customers included people with just a few accounts, to those with thousands.

We tied core features together in an activity dashboard, increasing daily engagement.

Challenges

Enterprise-level complexity

U.S. Bank supports many types of account types and entrypoints. In order to build the product into this system, I spoke to experts and studied credit systems deeply.

Broad customer-base

The platform needed to be adaptable for all dozens of different customer profiles. I conducted several user research studies to build and understand those profiles.

Many internal stakeholders

As a designer, I worked to bridge the gap between many cross-functional partners like Product/Compliance with visuals and prototyes. Communication was key.

Our transaction page allows users to add info like tags and receipts, while also creating quick auto accounting rules.

Responsive data tables

Our users work with large, dense datasets: transactions, cards, users, limits. On tablets and mobile, we needed to continue to show important info despite limited space.

For those screens, I removed decorative icons and changed to a list format.

Prioritized columns

On some screens, users are allowed to customize the amount of columns that are shown. This helps accountants review dense amounts of info, but can obscure critical cells.

I used a sticky column to make our Card actions visible at all times.

Filtering

I stored our many filter options inside a collapsible sidesheet for quick access.

Impact

With a few techniques applied, we unlocked clearer and faster data scanning for our power users. Additionally, mobile usage for heavy table access on the go became possible!

Making long forms digestible

Our product included a lot of long forms involving sensitive data like social security numbers (SSN), credit limits, and more. For example: our Add User flow requires more than 15 inputs!

I used a variety of UX fundamentals to make the flow more scannable, boosting comprehension and reducing drop-off rates.

Grouping

I broke the form into logical steps, grouping inputs that were related.

Progressive disclosure

For advanced options like our User Permissions (available only to users selected as administrators), I hid UI until it was needed.

Form structure

To boost scannability overall, I followed basic rules around visual comprehension: using left-aligned content, high contrast labels and replaced dropdowns with simpler components where possible.
