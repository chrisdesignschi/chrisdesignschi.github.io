Project background

What the product does

The product structure is pretty simple! Customers with an existing credit line with U.S. Bank can use the app to portion their credit into corporate cards for their employees.

Those cards can be customized with a variety of controls and limits, and monitored for reporting and compliance.


Key customer needs

1. Be able to instantly and easily create cards for my employees

2. Monitor activity and stop bad spend before it happens

3. Have a central place to save expenses for accounting

Product goals

1. Deepen customer relationships

As a software that creates new ways to transact with U.S. Bank, our product was key to developing customer primacy.

In short, the more time people spend with our product, the more likely they are to rely on the bank to handle their cash and credit. Our users spend 3.5x more with U.S. Bank than typical customers.


2. Drive user adoption

The product creates revenue via 1.) credit interchange fees and 2.) annual subscription dues. Thus, features were geared around encouraging daily usage.

Our card creation and management features had to be lightweight, easy to understand, and work for both business owners and employees


3. Design to scale

After acquisition, the complexity of our operations increased by multiple levels of complexity. I rebuilt flows around compliance regulations and dozens of new customer types.

I even redesigned our architecture from a debit-based model to credit-based! (credit limits, regulatory features, billing cycles)

My focuses

From startup to acquisition, I wore a lot of hats! I focused on developing product sophistication, simplifying complex processes, and improving usability.
