Creating a retail landing page for Superdry that breathes.

Retail Web

Superdry's eCommerce conversion rates spike when they frequently engage customers with new material.


I love the Superdry fashion brand. I wanted to redesign and code their retail site. My research showed Superdry's competitors succeeded by frequently engaging customers at a low-level online versus massive seasonal campaigns. I surmised that online, Superdry might improve sales the same way—thus, I built a site that focused on weekly engagement.

Why modular widgets?


My research showed that competitor streetwear brands like Supreme spiked in popularity during collaborations—for example, Google Trends showed mutual growth each time Nike and Supreme released a collab. Thus, I planned for my new site to take as much advantage of Superdry's collaborative works.

Thus, I resolved to create a landing page that was flexible around Superdry's fast-paced campaign schedule.

Swapping out modules: Frequency works!


Moreover, my research found that Superdry competitors found success when they "dripped" products, usually on a weekly basis. I proposed that Superdry modify the website on a more frequent basis, rather than through large collections that might fall to short attention spans.
